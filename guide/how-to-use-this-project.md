# How to Use This Project

Welcome to **Rediscovering Large Language Models**.

This project is not designed to be read like a traditional textbook. It is a learning journey built around curiosity, experimentation, and first-principles thinking. The objective is not simply to understand *what* modern AI systems do, but to understand *why* they work and *how* they were built.

![Project Overview](../assets/all.png)

## Before You Begin

You do **not** need prior experience in Machine Learning or Artificial Intelligence.

The only recommended prerequisites are:

* Basic Python programming
* High-school mathematics
* Curiosity and patience

Everything else is introduced progressively throughout the roadmap.

Instead of immediately reading the explanation, take time to think first.

# How to Study Each Topic

For every topic, follow these steps:

### 1. Read the Question

Understand what you are trying to learn.

Avoid searching for answers immediately.

---

### 2. Understand the Problem

Ask yourself:

* Why does this problem exist?
* Why couldn't previous approaches solve it?

Understanding the problem is often more valuable than memorizing the solution.

---

### 3. Create Your Own Hypothesis

Before reading the official explanation, write down your own prediction.

It does **not** need to be correct.

Incorrect hypotheses often lead to deeper understanding than correct guesses.

---

### 4. Learn the Theory

Now study the established explanation.

Compare it with your hypothesis.

Ask yourself:

* Which assumptions were correct?
* Which were incorrect?
* Why?

---

### 5. Build It

Understanding grows through implementation.

Whenever possible, implement the concept yourself instead of relying on existing libraries.

---

### 6. Experiment

Do not stop after making something work.

Change it.

Break it.

Remove components.

Observe what happens.

Many important intuitions come from failure rather than success.

---

### 7. Reflect

Finally, answer these questions:

* What did I actually understand?
* What are the limitations?
* Could I explain this to someone else?
* If I had to redesign it today, what would I change?

Reflection transforms information into understanding.

# Learn Actively

Avoid reading continuously without thinking.

Pause frequently and ask yourself questions.

Try to predict what comes next before reading the explanation.

The objective is not to finish the book quickly, but to develop a mental model that remains useful long after individual technologies have changed.

---

# Don't Memorize

Technologies evolve.

Frameworks change.

Models improve.

First principles remain.

Whenever you encounter a new concept, focus on understanding the problem it solves rather than memorizing its implementation.

---

# Take Your Time

This project is intentionally designed to be explored slowly.

There is no recommended pace.

Spend as much time as necessary on each topic until you genuinely understand it.

Deep understanding is always more valuable than fast progress.

---

# Keep Building

Reading creates knowledge.

Building creates understanding.

Experimentation creates intuition.

Reflection creates wisdom.

Whenever possible, implement every important idea yourself.

The final goal is not simply to know how modern AI works, but to develop the ability to reason about, improve, and create the next generation of intelligent systems.
